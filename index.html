Terry Pratchett and the Discworld

"Give a man a fire and he's warm for a day, but set fire to him and he's warm for the rest of his life"

Sir Terence David John Pratchett was an English author of fantasy novels and is best known for his Discworld series and a penchant for wearing large black fedora hats.

More than 85 million books sold worldwide.

Knighted for services to literature.

Received the World Fantasy Award for Life Achievement.

Celebrated in style with a pint of Guinness after receiving an honourary degree from an Irish University

Kept only carnivorous plants in his greenhouse

  • 1948
  • A delivery by stork to David and Eileen Pratchett
  • 1968
  • Married Lyn Purves
  • 1971
  • First ever novel published – The Carpet People
  • 1976
  • Daughter Rhianna delivered by stork
  • 1983
  • A fossil sea-turtle was named Psephophorus terrypratchetti in his honour by the palaeontologist Richard Koehler
  • 1995
  • First Discworld novel published
  • 2009
  • Was Knighted.. and later forged his own sword!
  • 2015
  • DEATH arrives for Sir Terry
  • 2017
  • The HDD from his PC with all unfinished works was destroyed by a steamroller as per his wishes
